PartsOfSpeech
     

We live in an increasingly connected world where advancing technologies and the internet have enabled us to communicate with one another no matter where we are. With an ever more integrated world economy, multilingualism is on the rise. Language, aside from being a form of communication also affords us a means of accessing different cultures and relating to each other on a deeper and personal level. Nelson Mandela once said, "If you talk to a man in a language he understands, that goes to his head. If you talk to him in his language, that goes to his heart.”

    What are the parts of speech?

  • adjective a word belonging to one of the major form classes in any of numerous languages and typically serving as a modifier of a noun to denote a quality of the thing named, to indicate its quantity or extent, or to specify a thing as distinct from something else
    // The word "red" in "the red car" is an adjective.
  • adposition an umbrella term for prepositions and postpositions:
     preposition a word—and almost always a very small, very common word—that shows direction ("to" in "a letter to you"), location ("at" in "at the door"), or time ("by" in "by noon"), or that introduces an object ("of" in "a basket of apples"); prepositions are typically followed by an object, which can be a noun (noon), a noun phrase (the door), or a pronoun (you)
     postposition a word or morpheme placed after the word it governs
     // The morpheme "-ward" in "homeward" and the word "ago" in "ten years ago" are postpositions.
  • adverb a word that modifies or qualifies verbs, adjectives, other adverbs, phrases, or even entire sentences
    In “arrived early,” “runs slowly,” “stayed home,” and “works hard” the words “early,” “slowly,” “home,” and “hard” are adverbs.
  • affix one or more sounds or letters occurring as a bound form attached to the beginning or end of a word, base, or phrase or inserted within a word or base and serving to produce a derivative word or an inflectional form
    // The affix in the word "attendance" is "-ance."
  • conjunction an uninflected linguistic form that joins together sentences, clauses, phrases, or words
    // Some common conjunctions are "and," "but," and "although."
  • determiner a modifying word (such as "a," "the," "some," "any," "my," or "your") that comes before a noun or noun group and is used to show which thing is being referred to
  • noun a word that refers to a thing (book), a person (Betty Crocker), an animal (cat), a place (Omaha), a quality (softness), an idea (justice), or an action (yodeling); it's usually a single word, but not always - cake, shoes, school bus, and time and a half are all nouns
  • numeral a word or phrase that describes a numerical quantity
  • pronoun any of a small set of words in a language that are used as substitutes for nouns or noun phrases and whose referents are named or understood in the context
    // "I", "you", "she", "this", and "it" are pronouns.
  • particle a unit of speech expressing some general aspect of meaning or some connective or limiting relation and including most prepositions and conjunctions, and some interjections and adverbs
    // The word "up" in "he ate up all his dinner" is a particle.
  • punctuation the marks (such as periods and commas) in a piece of writing that make its meaning clear and that separate it into sentences, clauses, etc
  • verb a word that shows an action (sing), occurrence (develop), or state of being (exist)
